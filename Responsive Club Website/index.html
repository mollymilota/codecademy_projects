Crown Heights Ornithology Collective

About Us

On a Saturday morning in 2014, Dame Judi Finch and Kanye Nest crossed paths at Brower Park hoping to catch a glipse of a rumoured Great Horned Owl. That meeting was the start of a beautiful friendship and also the first gathering of what is now known as the Crown Heights Ornithology Collective.

Today, CHOCo is made up of more than 50 regular members who come together for weekly walks and the ocassional upstate juant. Whether you’re an experienced birder or newly bird-curious, the collective has something for you!

Programming

Walks

Join us at the Brooklyn Ave entrance on Tuesdays at 8:00 am and Saturdays at 7:30 am. All experience levels are welcome. Bring a friend!

Field Trips

We love our neighborhood and even we like to stretch our wings. Join us for one of our upstate trips to expand your birding purview. Advanced registration is required for all trips.

Classes

Held monthly, our Birding 101 class is a great way to get your bearings. Learn about binocular basics, field guides, citizen science, and when and where to get the best views. Classes are donation based and available on a first-come first-serve basis.

Lectures

Talk Birdy To Me is a quarterly lecture series featuring traveling ornithologists, neighborhood experts, and friends from other NYC bird clubs. Trust us when we say you won’t want to miss it.
